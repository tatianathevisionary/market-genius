Why Bitcoin Moves — The Driver Model

synthesized from BlackRock crypto-volatility research, market analyses & current coverage (Jun 2026)
DriverTimescaleHow it moves priceWatch via
1. Global liquidity & ratesmonths–years The big one. Cheap money → risk appetite → BTC up. Rate hikes / QT drain liquidity → BTC down. BTC pays no yield, so when safe yields rise, holding it costs more. 10Y yield, DXY, Fed odds (A-score)
2. Flows (ETF / institutions)days–months Since spot ETFs, big money enters/exits in measurable daily flows. One $1.26B block sale moved the whole market in May. ETF net flows (B3)
3. Leverage cascadesminutes–days Traders borrow to amplify bets. A small drop forces liquidations → forced selling → bigger drop → more liquidations. This is why BTC falls 10% in hours "on no news" ($1.8B wiped this week). funding rates, open interest (B2)
4. Supply mechanicsyears Fixed 21M cap; halvings cut new issuance every 4y. Slow structural tailwind — sets the cycle rhythm, not the daily candle. halving calendar
5. Narrative & sentimentweeks–months BTC is a momentum asset: capital chases whatever story is hottest. Right now the AI/IPO narrative is draining speculative capital from crypto (the Schwab thesis). Reddit buckets, dominance, news wire
6. Cost-basis behaviorweeks–months Holders defend break-even: buyers above $90k capitulating now = late-bear signature; "made my money back, I'm out" selling caps rallies at prior highs. on-chain cohorts (B4), S/R levels
7. Shocks (geo/regulatory)instant Strait of Hormuz, ETF approvals, exchange failures. Unpredictable by definition — the system reacts via the transmission chain rather than predicts. news wire, oil (A1)
THE SYNTHESIS: daily moves = leverage + flows + sentiment (layers 2,3,5). Monthly trends = liquidity + narrative (1,5). Multi-year cycles = liquidity + supply (1,4). When you see a move, ask which timescale it belongs to before reacting — most retail mistakes are answering a days-layer move with a years-layer decision, or vice versa.
