Whale School

WHY THIS PAGE EXISTS

WHAT IS A WHALE? Any entity whose single order is big enough to move price by itself — funds, ETF market-makers, miners, corporate treasuries, exchanges rebalancing, and OG holders. Bitcoin ownership is extremely concentrated: a small fraction of addresses controls most supply, so what a handful of large players do matters more than what a million small wallets think.

WHY THEY MATTER. Two reasons. Mechanical: a $5M market order eats through the order book and moves price directly — clusters of large prints mark exactly where serious money defends a level or unloads into one. Informational: large players often act on flows and research you can't see; their aggregate footprint on the tape is the closest free proxy to "smart money" positioning. Retail sentiment (see SOCIAL) tells you what the crowd feels; the whale tape tells you what big money does. When the two disagree, trust the tape.

THE FOUR SIGNATURES.
🟢 Absorption — heavy BUY prints at a support zone while price holds: someone is buying everything panickers throw at them. Bullish; strongest when it lines up with a confluence-rated support on the console.
🔴 Distribution — heavy SELL prints into rallies while price stalls at resistance: unloading into strength. Bearish, and the polite cousin of a rug.
⚡ Capitulation sweep — massive sells, price plunges, then a long lower wick on high volume: forced sellers met by whale bids. This is the B1 signature in SIGNALS.md — historically where bottoms get made.
🟣 Exchange flows — big on-chain transfers TO exchanges often precede selling (coins staged for sale); big withdrawals suggest cold-storage accumulation. The system now auto-classifies these by checking both sides of every transfer against a book of known exchange wallets (Binance, Bitfinex, OKX, Crypto.com, BitMEX, gate.io…) plus a behavioral check for repeat service wallets:
  INFLOW = coins arrived at a known exchange wallet → sell-side supply up.
  OUTFLOW = coins left a known exchange wallet → toward private custody.
  SHUFFLE = exchange↔exchange or repeat service wallet → housekeeping, ignore.
  UNKNOWN = neither address recognized — most retail deposit addresses are fresh and unlabel-able with free data, so unknowns stay honest unknowns. The labeled wallets live in data/exchange_addresses.json — add any address to improve coverage.

THE BOOK GROWS ITSELF (CIOH). The 21 seed wallets are just a starting point. Every Bitcoin transaction reveals ownership for free via the Common Input Ownership Heuristic: all the addresses spent as inputs to one transaction are controlled by the same entity (only one party holds all the keys). So when a known Binance wallet co-spends alongside three unlabeled addresses, those three are Binance — the system records them and they're labeled forever after. This is the exact foundation every chain-analytics firm (Chainalysis, Arkham, Elementus) is built on; combined with the behavioral and seed-list checks, stacked heuristics like these reach ~94% labeling accuracy in the forensics literature. The seed list is cold wallets that move rarely, so learning is slow at first — but it compounds, and unlike a static list it never goes stale.

HOW IT'S CAPTURED. Exchange-wallet flows are caught in real time: a websocket holds a live line to mempool.space and reacts the instant any labeled wallet transacts, so an exchange deposit or withdrawal lands here within seconds — no 5-minute sampling gap. Transfers between two unlabeled wallets (the UNKNOWN bucket) are still picked up by the 5-min sampler, since a complete free firehose of every transaction no longer exists. So: exchange flow = real-time and complete; anonymous-to-anonymous = sampled. The exchange flow is the part that carries signal, so that's the part we made airtight.

WHERE B-GRADE ANALYSTS GET FOOLED: one print is noise — read dominance over hours, not single events. An aggressive buy spike can be a short squeeze, not accumulation. An exchange withdrawal can be an internal wallet migration, not a customer pulling coins. A-grade reads come from confluence: tape dominance + an S/R level + the regime on the console — never one signal alone.
